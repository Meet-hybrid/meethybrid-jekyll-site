About Me

From Business Student
to Software Engineer

A founder story, a learning journey, and a life still being written.

The Beginning — Business, People & How the World Works

My journey didn't start with code. It started with questions. Growing up in Nigeria, I was always fascinated by how systems work — how businesses grow, how people behave, how organizations succeed or collapse under their own weight. That curiosity led me to study Business Management, where I spent years learning about economics, psychology, entrepreneurship, and the mechanics of human decision-making.

That foundation gave me something most engineers don't have early in their career: a deep understanding of why things are built, not just how.

The Pivot — Discovering Software Engineering

The shift happened gradually, then all at once. I started noticing that the most transformative businesses in the world were technology companies. Not because technology was cool — but because technology was the lever. It could multiply effort. It could reach millions. It could build things that worked while you slept.

I started learning to code. Python first. Then JavaScript. Then React. The feeling of writing something that actually works — something a real person can use — was unlike anything else I'd experienced. I was hooked.

Semicolon Africa — Learning to Ship

Joining Semicolon Africa as a Software Engineering Intern was the moment everything became real. No more tutorials. No more "hello world." Suddenly I was writing production React features, building Node.js APIs, working in Agile sprints, submitting pull requests to a real team, and having my code reviewed by engineers who expected quality.

It was intense. It was also exactly what I needed. I learned how to read and navigate a real codebase, how to write tests that actually protect the system, how to communicate clearly in engineering discussions, and how to ship with confidence. The gap between "learning to code" and "building software professionally" is wide — Semicolon Africa pushed me across it.

The Cybersecurity Chapter

As I grew as a software engineer, I became increasingly interested in the security layer. Every API I built raised a question: how would someone attack this? Every system I worked on made me wonder: what are its weak points?

That curiosity turned into a structured learning roadmap. Today I'm actively studying Linux, networking fundamentals, ethical hacking, and security tools — with a clear target: professional competency in cybersecurity to complement my engineering work. In the digital economy, building and securing are two sides of the same coin.

Psychology, People & Product Thinking

One thing my business education gave me that I'll never trade is a genuine interest in human psychology. Why do people use the products they use? What motivates behavior? What makes something feel intuitive versus frustrating?

This shapes how I approach product and engineering work. I care about the user sitting on the other side of the interface — not just the function call that powers it. That combination of technical skill and human empathy is what I mean when I say I build with a human-centered lens.

The Writing Life

In parallel with everything else, I write. My first book, The Lie We Were Taught, is a thought-provoking exploration of life, growth, spirituality, and what it truly means to be human. It's personal, it's philosophical, and it came from years of reading, questioning, and reflecting.

The Learning To Be Human series is just beginning. More books are in the pipeline — each one an attempt to give language to the questions most people feel but rarely articulate.

What I'm Building Toward

My long-term goal is clear: build technology products that meaningfully improve how people connect, learn, and grow. That might look like a SaaS product, a startup, a developer tool, or something that doesn't have a name yet. What matters is the impact — the person whose life is measurably better because of something I built.

I'm open to remote opportunities and international relocation. I want to work on hard problems with talented people, keep learning at a ridiculous pace, and earn the right to eventually build something entirely my own.

That's the story. It's still being written.
