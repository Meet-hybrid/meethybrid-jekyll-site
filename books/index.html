The Lie We Were Taught
// Book 1 of the Learning To Be Human Series

A thought-provoking exploration of the stories we're told about life — by our families, institutions, religions, and cultures — and the quiet work of unlearning what doesn't serve us.

The Lie We Were Taught moves through questions of identity, purpose, spiritual growth, and what it genuinely means to be human in a world that constantly tries to define that for you. It's part memoir, part philosophy, part invitation — written for anyone who has ever suspected that the life they were handed wasn't quite the right fit.

Author's Note

I write because some questions don't have easy answers — and the attempt to articulate them is itself a form of growth.

Every book in this series comes from a place of genuine wrestling. Not performance. Not theory for theory's sake. These are the questions I've sat with at 2am, the observations I couldn't stop turning over, the things I needed to say out loud.

If even one person reads something here and feels less alone in their questions, then it was worth writing.
